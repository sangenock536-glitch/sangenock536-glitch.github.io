Affiliate Disclosure

Last updated: June 1, 2025

FreeStudyHub is committed to being transparent with our readers. This page explains how this website earns money and how (or whether) that influences the content we publish.

What Are Affiliate Links?

Some links on FreeStudyHub are "affiliate links". This means that if you click a link to a third-party product or service and then make a purchase or sign up, we may receive a small commission at no extra cost to you.

Affiliate commissions help us cover the costs of running this website — hosting, domain registration, and the time spent testing and writing about free study tools.

How We Use Affiliate Links

Affiliate links on FreeStudyHub are typically placed on:

  • Tool review pages (e.g., "Try Free Version" or "Visit [Tool Name]" buttons)
  • Top Tools list pages
  • Blog posts that reference specific tools

Where a link is an affiliate link, we aim to indicate this clearly with a label or a disclosure notice near the link or at the top of the page.

Does This Affect Our Recommendations?

No. Our editorial opinions are not for sale.

We choose which tools to feature based entirely on whether they are genuinely useful, genuinely free, and genuinely good for students. We do not:

  • Rank a tool higher because it pays a higher commission
  • Give a positive review in exchange for payment
  • Accept sponsored content disguised as editorial reviews
  • Remove honest criticisms from reviews at a company's request

We regularly review tools that do not have affiliate programmes. In those cases, we use standard outbound links. The quality of our review is the same regardless of whether a tool has an affiliate arrangement with us.

Advertising

FreeStudyHub may display third-party advertisements via Google AdSense or similar networks. These ads are clearly marked as "Advertisement" or similar. Ad placements do not influence the editorial content of this site.

FTC Compliance

FreeStudyHub complies with the United States Federal Trade Commission (FTC) guidelines on disclosure of affiliate relationships and sponsored content. If you are outside the US, similar disclosure requirements may apply in your country and we aim to comply with those as well.

For more information on the FTC guidelines, see: ftc.gov.
