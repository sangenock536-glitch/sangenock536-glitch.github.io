What is Notion?

Notion is a productivity and note-taking platform that launched in 2016. It lets you create pages that combine text, images, tables, code blocks, and more into a single flexible document. You can use it as a simple note-taker, a project management tool, a database, or all three at once.

The free plan ("Personal") is generous enough for most individual students. You get unlimited pages and blocks, basic sharing, and access to every core feature — with a few limits on collaboration and file uploads.

Who is Notion Best For?

Notion works best for students who:

  • Want to organise all their subjects in one place
  • Work on group projects that need a shared wiki
  • Prefer customising how their workspace looks
  • Take structured, hierarchical notes
  • Need a dashboard to track deadlines and tasks

It's probably not the best fit if you need to work fully offline, prefer pen-and-paper-style handwriting, or find complex software overwhelming. In that case, check out Google Docs or Obsidian.

Pros and Cons

✅ What We Like

  • Extremely flexible — build any layout you want
  • Beautiful template gallery to get started fast
  • Works on Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, and the web
  • Real-time collaboration with classmates
  • Free plan is genuinely powerful
  • Integrates with Google Calendar, Slack, and more

❌ What to Watch Out For

  • The blank canvas can feel overwhelming at first
  • Offline mode is limited and unreliable
  • Can slow down with very large databases
  • Free plan limits guest collaboration
  • File upload limited to 5 MB on free plan
